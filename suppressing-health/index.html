Cures are a one-time transaction. Chronic disease management is a recurring revenue model. The pharmaceutical industry's fiduciary obligation is to shareholders, not patients. A healthy population is, from a pure revenue perspective, a shrinking addressable market.
$1.5 trillion

Annual global pharmaceutical revenue. Recurring, if you structure it correctly.
The Playbook Price at Maximum Extraction

Life-saving drugs should be priced at what the market will bear. The market, in this case, is people who will die without them. The willingness-to-pay is, by definition, total.
Evergreen Patents

File incremental modification patents to block generic entry. A slightly different molecular structure, a new delivery mechanism, a combination pill — each resets the clock on exclusivity. The generic threat is a 20-year problem you can extend to 40.
Create New Conditions

Fund research that identifies, names, and pathologizes new conditions requiring new proprietary treatments. Restless leg syndrome was not a public health crisis until it had a drug. Direct-to-consumer advertising creates demand.
Block TRIPS Waivers

Intellectual property protection is sacrosanct. Every generic produced in India or Brazil is revenue foregone. Frame opposition as "protecting innovation incentives."
Direct-to-Consumer Advertising

Legal in the U.S. and New Zealand. "Ask your doctor about..." campaigns create demand for products people didn't know they needed. Remarkably effective at expanding the addressable market beyond the clinically indicated population.
Implementation Roadmap
  1. Conduct willingness-to-pay analyses for life-sustaining therapies
  2. Engage patent strategy firms to identify evergreening opportunities
  3. Develop DTC advertising campaigns with focus-grouped condition naming
  4. Establish lobbying positions against compulsory licensing provisions
  5. Fund patient advocacy organizations that align with product promotion goals
