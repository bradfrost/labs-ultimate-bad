An informed populace is an unpredictable variable. Critical thinking creates consumers who comparison-shop, voters who read policy, and workers who organize. Each of these outcomes represents a friction cost. Information asymmetry is a competitive advantage — manage the supply accordingly.

Children out of school. Each one a future compliant consumer.
The Playbook Defund Public Education

Redirect funding to private alternatives with selective enrollment. The result: a two-tier system where quality education is available to those who can pay, and a credential mill for everyone else. Frame it as "school choice."
Flood the Information Space

Not lies, exactly — just noise. Make it impossible to distinguish signal from noise at scale. When everything is equally questionable, nothing is credible, and the loudest voice wins.
Underpay Teachers

Keep teacher compensation below market until the profession attracts only those who can't do otherwise, or those too idealistic to notice. The quality decline is gradual and deniable.
Oppose Critical Thinking Curricula

Frame analytical thinking education as ideological indoctrination. "Parents' rights" and "curriculum transparency" are effective packaging. The goal is to produce graduates who consume information rather than evaluate it.
Education as Debt Trap

Make higher education so expensive it functions as a financial indenture rather than a ladder. Student debt creates compliant workers who can't afford to take risks, start businesses, or organize.
Implementation Roadmap
  1. Identify school board elections with low turnout — high leverage, low cost
  2. Fund media properties that prioritize engagement over accuracy
  3. Develop "curriculum review" legislation templates for sympathetic legislators
  4. Lobby against teacher compensation increases at state and local levels
  5. Oppose student debt relief legislation — protect the debt instrument
